Mi Lan Xiang Feng Xi Review

May 29, 2020
1 min read

This is my first dan cong style tea, where the tea bushes are not trimmed, and allowed to grow ‘wild’. Most teas come from tea plants that are trimmed, cut and grafted to maintain the teas fragrance while the dan cong style teas produce intense and flavorful teas.
This oolong is comes from the Fengxi village in the Phoenix mountains, home of the dan cong style. It’s name translates to “Honey Orchid Fragrance”, and is a flagship of modern dan cong style teas.

tea: Mi Lan Xiang Feng Xi
type: loose leaf oolong tea, dan cong
tea house: Camellia Sinensis

The oolong brews into a dark, orange brownish liquor. It has an extremely fruity flavour as well as flowery notes - it’s taste almost reminds me of lychee and peony white tea, despite it being a heavily oxidized oolong. It also leaves a sweet after taste, much like what honey does.

Overall rating: 7/10, normally not a big fan of heavily oxidized oolongs, but this one impressed me
